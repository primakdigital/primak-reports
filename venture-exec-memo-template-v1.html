Usage Notes
How to Use This Template

Structure (10 sections + 3 appendices):

  1. Executive Summary — Verdict + 4 cards (What, Why Now, Investment, Key Risk)
  2. Problem & Opportunity — Market gap, gaps table
  3. Validation Evidence — Comparables, market numbers
  4. Business Model — Revenue model, unit economics
  5. Competitive Landscape — Competitor map, positioning
  6. Legal & IP — Traffic light, budget by phase (delete if N/A)
  7. Technology Layer — Tech stack, costs (delete if N/A)
  8. Risk Register — 5-7 risks with mitigations
  9. Roadmap — 3 phases max
  10. Go/No-Go Decision — Scored criteria table + verdict
  11. Appendix A: Pilot — MANDATORY. Kill/Continue/Scale criteria.
  12. Appendix B: Roles — Who owns what
  13. Appendix C: Sources — All cited references

Evidence Classification (mandatory on every data point):

  • V Verified Fact — Cited from a specific source. Traceable to Appendix C.
  • E Reasoned Estimate — Calculated or derived from verified data. Show your math.
  • U Unknown — Assumption. Must be validated by the pilot or further research.

Go/No-Go Logic:

  • GO: All criteria PASS (evidence = V or strong E)
  • CONDITIONAL GO: No FAILs, 1-2 CONDITIONAL items resolvable by pilot
  • NO-GO: Any critical FAIL, or 3+ CONDITIONAL items

Formatting Rules:

  • English only. LTR. Word-friendly fonts (Segoe UI, Calibri).
  • Version number in title and footer. Increment on every revision.
  • Print-friendly: instruction blocks hidden on print.
  • Max 1 scroll per section. If a section is too long, split it.
  • Can export to Word/PDF via browser print or pandoc.

Opportunity Cost Gate (evaluate before writing the memo):

  • Does Yaniv need to be involved? If yes, how many hours/week?
  • Does this compete with Primak's core pipeline for Yaniv's time?
  • If the answer to both is "significant" — the venture needs an operations partner or it's a NO-GO regardless of market potential.
