06 — Premium Playbooks (WS4)

What the winners do differently

We studied 8 fractional executives who cracked premium positioning ($15K+ engagements). Five patterns emerged.

Pattern 1 — They Don't Say "Fractional"

Melissa Perri: "Board Member. CEO Advisor." Rich Mironov: "Interim CPO." April Dunford: "Positioning Consultant." None of the premium practitioners use the word "fractional" as their primary identifier. It either doesn't appear or is buried in body copy. The word signals discount, not premium.

Pattern 2 — Diagnosis Is The Product

April Dunford charges $50K-$100K for a workshop, not a deliverable. Blair Enns says: "Free proposals = devalued thinking." Every premium practitioner makes the DIAGNOSTIC step a paid engagement. The pattern: make diagnosis the first sale, not a free sample.

Pattern 3 — Published Authority

Melissa Perri (2 books), April Dunford (1 canonical book), Blair Enns ($990 manifesto), Asia Orangio (1 book). 4 of 8 premium practitioners wrote a book. The book is not the revenue source — it's the credibility compression device. It replaces 100 credentials with 1.

Pattern 4 — Visible Floor, Invisible Ceiling

April Dunford publishes $50K minimum on her site. Nobody argues, nobody haggles. Visible minimum pricing self-selects buyers before the first call. The wrong prospects leave. The right ones open at the right level. Not publishing prices = training the market to negotiate down.

Pattern 5 — Qualification Built Into Positioning

Mark Donnigan: 1.5-3 year minimum. April Dunford: "smart, opinionated teams." Melissa Perri: no public pricing at all. The qualification happens BEFORE the sales conversation through positioning language, not during the call.

Jody H-P
Context
"The single most actionable finding: April Dunford publishes her price. Sivan Kadosh publishes his price. One charges $50K-$100K. The other charges $8K/month. The difference isn't talent — it's positioning. The number you publish becomes the number people believe you're worth."

1
One-Liner
Buyers search for symptoms, not credentials. "Fractional CPO" is seller language. The most effective message maps from buyer pain to capability. 92.8% of clients come through referrals — the one-liner must be repeatable by others.
The one-liner must survive paraphrasing. If someone can't retell it at a dinner party, it fails. Test: can a non-product person repeat it?
2
Title
"Fractional CPO" is searchable but devaluing. Premium practitioners avoid it (Perri, Mironov, Dunford). BUT — LinkedIn search needs it somewhere. Mironov says only "interim" works. Israel has zero representation.
The title must work on LinkedIn search WITHOUT making "fractional" the primary identity. Put it in the About/Experience, not the headline. The Israeli angle is an untapped SEO advantage.
3
Services
Premium practitioners make diagnosis the paid first step (Dunford: $50K workshop). "Scan/Sprint/Residency" mirrors generic competitor language (Kadosh, Kritikos use similar). One offering with scope variants outperforms a branded menu for solo practitioners.
The entry product IS the qualification gate. If diagnosis is free, Primak's thinking is devalued. Service names should be invisible — the brand is Primak, not the package.
4
Proof Cases
CathWorks is not publicly connected to Yaniv anywhere. The $585M acquisition is live news (Feb 3, 2026). The window to publish is closing. Harpal Singh dominates because of 13 published case studies. Primak has 5 cases but zero are indexed.
Publish the CathWorks story THIS WEEK. One LinkedIn article > any amount of brand strategy. The proof cases don't work if they only live in proposals — they must be findable online.
5
Verticals
"High-stakes AI" is real (Google Research, MIT, EU AI Act). Not a Primak invention. Maps to a $90B+ market. Nobody owns it as positioning. EU compliance deadline Aug 2026 = immediate demand.
"High-stakes AI" validated. But the term works as a FILTER (conversation tool), not a search category (nobody searches for it yet). Content is needed to CREATE the category — articles, posts, a manifesto.
6
Voice
Premium practitioners use numbers as proof (Singh: "doubled revenue," "10x ARR," "zero churn"). The Chill Test already exists in Primak's system. Buyer testimonials show what clients value: "challenged our ideas," "different perspectives," "didn't settle."
The voice must include numbers always. But add a new dimension: the CHALLENGER voice. Buyers hired these people to be challenged, not comforted. "We challenged their ideas, even as founders" — that's the voice buyers pay premium for.
7
Deal Gates
Premium practitioners qualify structurally: public pricing (Dunford), engagement minimums (Donnigan), narrow ICP (Orangio). Mironov's most powerful gate: "I hire my own replacement" — signals no conflict of interest. No gate = price war.
Keep the gates. Add one: the Mironov gate — "I build the product function so it doesn't need me." This signals confidence and removes the buyer's biggest fear (dependency).
