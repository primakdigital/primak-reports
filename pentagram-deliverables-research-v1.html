The Complete Pentagram Brand Identity Playbook

Every phase, every deliverable, every file format. Researched from real Pentagram projects: Mastercard, Slack, Warner Bros., Channel 4, Calendly, Cohere, Mozilla, The New School, ERM, Fashion for Good, Satalia.
v1 — 23 February 2026 — Primak Brand Reset Reference
01 — Process

How Pentagram Actually Works: Phase by Phase

Pentagram does not follow a cookie-cutter process. Each partner runs their own studio within the firm. But across documented projects (Mastercard, Channel 4, Slack, Calendly, Mozilla, Warner Bros.), a consistent 6-phase structure emerges. The key difference from regular agencies: a Pentagram partner (not a junior account manager) leads every phase personally.

01

Immersion & Discovery

Deep research into the brand's mission, history, competitive landscape, audience, and cultural context. Pentagram partners personally interview stakeholders, audit every touchpoint, and analyze how the brand is experienced by customers. For Warner Bros., this meant studying 100 years of heritage. For Mastercard, understanding the shift from physical cards to digital payments.

  • Stakeholder interviews (C-suite, board, customers)
  • Competitive & cultural landscape audit
  • Brand experience mapping (every touchpoint)
  • Heritage & equity assessment
  • Audience research & persona development
Duration: 2-6 weeks
02

Strategy & Positioning

Distillation of research into a strategic framework. Brand essence, positioning, messaging architecture, tone of voice. Pentagram bridges strategy and execution; they reject strategy as "words on a whiteboard" and prove how principles translate into tangible design. For Fashion for Good, this produced a 3-word manifesto: "Only good fashion."

  • Brand essence / core idea definition
  • Positioning statement
  • Messaging platform (hierarchy of messages)
  • Tone of voice guidelines
  • Brand principles / attributes
  • Naming (when applicable)
Duration: 2-4 weeks
03

Concept Development

Multiple creative directions explored. Pentagram typically presents 2-4 distinct concepts, each with a core idea, preliminary mark, type direction, and mood. For Slack, Michael Bierut explored multiple approaches before landing on the pinwheel. For Channel 4, the team tested "portals, magic, and time machines" before settling on the "traveller" metaphor.

  • 2-4 distinct creative directions
  • Preliminary marks and type explorations
  • Mood boards and visual territories
  • Concept rationale documents
  • Client presentation and collaborative selection
Duration: 3-6 weeks
04

Design Development

Selected concept refined into a complete identity system. This is the heaviest phase. Logo finalized, typography selected or commissioned, color system calibrated, visual language built, patterns and graphic tools developed. For Mastercard, hundreds of color tests were conducted. For Channel 4, color selection alone took 4+ months.

  • Logo refinement (geometry, proportions, optical corrections)
  • Custom typeface design or selection
  • Color system calibration (Pantone, CMYK, RGB, HEX)
  • Visual language: patterns, shapes, textures, illustration
  • Icon system development
  • Photography direction
  • Motion/animation principles
  • Application mockups across key touchpoints
Duration: 6-12 weeks
05

System Build & Guidelines

The identity is codified into a brand book and toolkit. Rules for usage, clear space, minimum sizes, do/don't examples. Templates for recurring applications. Digital toolkits (Figma libraries, parametric tools). For Cohere, Pentagram delivered custom Figma plugins and Cinema 4D tools. For Channel 4, a parametric motion toolkit.

  • Brand guidelines document (40-100+ pages)
  • Logo usage rules and clear space specs
  • Digital asset library (all formats)
  • Template suite (presentations, social, documents)
  • Figma/design tool component libraries
  • Parametric/generative design tools (when applicable)
Duration: 3-6 weeks
06

Implementation & Rollout

Deployment across all touchpoints: digital, print, environmental, product. Pentagram often directs the rollout, coordinating with specialist studios for motion, sound, environmental design, and web development. For Channel 4, they coordinated 6+ external studios. For The New School, they oversaw signage, environmental graphics, campus installations, and website redesign.

  • Website design/art direction
  • Environmental and signage implementation
  • Stationery and print production
  • Digital platform skins (apps, dashboards)
  • Launch campaign materials
  • Internal training and brand onboarding
Duration: 4-12 weeks

03 — Deliverables

The Complete Pentagram Deliverables List

Every single item Pentagram would deliver for a full brand identity engagement, compiled from 11 real projects. Organized by category, with file formats, purpose, and project examples.

A. Brand Strategy Deliverables

Deliverable What It Is Formats Why It Matters / Example
Brand Essence / Core Idea One sentence or phrase that captures the brand's fundamental truth. The strategic north star from which everything derives. PDF, Keynote/PPT Fashion for Good: "Only good fashion." Calendly: "Easy ahead." This is the seed of the entire system.
Positioning Statement Formal articulation of who the brand serves, what it offers, how it's different, and why it matters. 1-2 paragraphs. PDF, Word/Google Docs Mastercard: repositioned from "credit card company" to "forward-thinking, people-centered technology company."
Messaging Platform Hierarchy of messages: tagline, elevator pitch, long-form narrative, key messages by audience. Structured so different contexts get different messages from the same framework. PDF, Keynote/PPT Fashion for Good: 5 messaging pillars (Materials, Economy, Energy, Water, Lives). Mozilla: "Reclaim the internet."
Tone of Voice Guidelines Defines how the brand speaks: vocabulary, sentence structure, personality traits, do/don't examples, writing samples across channels (web, email, social, formal). PDF (part of brand book) Fashion for Good: "brave, wise, accessible." Satalia: dual tone (client-facing warm vs. recruitment-facing technical).
Brand Principles / Attributes 3-6 core principles that define brand behavior. Not just words: each principle comes with behavioral descriptions and design implications. PDF, Keynote/PPT Channel 4: behavioural principles that connect the identity across all platforms.
Naming (if applicable) Brand name, sub-brand naming convention, product naming system. Includes linguistic testing and trademark screening. PDF, Keynote/PPT Citi: Pentagram recommended unifying Citibank + Travelers under the 4-letter name "Citi."

B. Logo System

Deliverable What It Is Formats Why It Matters / Example
Primary Logo (Full Lockup) The main logo: symbol + wordmark together. This is the default version used in most contexts. AI, EPS, SVG, PDF, PNG (transparent, multiple sizes) Mastercard: interlocking circles + "mastercard" wordmark. Slack: pinwheel + "slack" logotype.
Symbol / Icon (Standalone) The mark without the wordmark. Used for favicons, app icons, small-space applications, merchandise. AI, EPS, SVG, PDF, PNG, ICO (favicon) Mastercard's wordless mark (used without brand name, recognized by 81% of consumers). Calendly's hollow "C."
Wordmark (Standalone) The brand name in its proprietary typographic treatment, without the symbol. Used in editorial, text-heavy, and co-branding contexts. AI, EPS, SVG, PDF, PNG Slack's lowercase sans-serif logotype. Warner Bros.' custom "Warner Bros." lettering.
Logo Variations Full color, single color (black), reversed (white on dark), grayscale. Horizontal, stacked, and compact configurations. Typically 8-16 total variations. AI, EPS, SVG, PDF, PNG per variation Every Pentagram project delivers multiple configurations tested against real contexts.
Clear Space & Minimum Size Rules Specifications for the protected area around the logo and the smallest size it can be reproduced at while remaining legible. Documented in brand guidelines (PDF) Critical for consistency. Warner Bros. shield was "optimized to perform across various platforms and scales."
Incorrect Usage Examples Visual guide showing how NOT to use the logo: no stretching, no color changes, no drop shadows, no rotation, etc. Usually 8-15 "don't" examples. Documented in brand guidelines (PDF) Standard in every Pentagram brand book. Prevents brand dilution by third parties.
Co-Branding Rules How the logo appears alongside partner/client logos. Size ratios, spacing, color priority. Documented in brand guidelines (PDF) Fashion for Good: partner logo colorways that adapt to each partnership context.

C. Typography System

Deliverable What It Is Formats Why It Matters / Example
Primary Typeface The main typeface for headlines, body, and brand communications. Can be custom-commissioned (bespoke) or a carefully selected existing font. Pentagram frequently commissions bespoke typefaces from type foundries. OTF, TTF, WOFF/WOFF2 (web), Variable Font files The New School: custom "Neue" by Peter Bil'ak. Cohere: 3 custom typefaces (Text, Outline, Mono). Warner Bros.: custom "Warner Bros. Sans."
Secondary Typeface Complementary typeface for supporting text, captions, or technical content. Provides contrast and hierarchy alongside the primary face. OTF, TTF, WOFF/WOFF2 ERM: Spezia (serif, primary) + Afrika Sans (wordmark) + Figtree (geometric supporting). Calendly: Gilroy (primary) + Poppins + Arial (supporting).
Type Scale & Hierarchy Specific size, weight, and line-height specifications for each level of typography: H1-H6, body, caption, label, button. Both print and digital scales. Documented in brand guidelines (PDF) + Figma component library Defines the exact visual rhythm of all communications. Different scales for print vs. digital.
Pairing Rules Guidelines for how typefaces work together: which combinations are allowed, relative sizing, when to use serif vs. sans-serif, etc. Documented in brand guidelines (PDF) Prevents ad-hoc font mixing that erodes brand coherence.
Font Licenses Commercial licenses for all typefaces, covering desktop, web, app, and server use. If custom, perpetual usage rights. License documentation (PDF) A real cost item. Custom typeface commissions range $10,000-$100,000+. Commercial font licenses $500-$5,000.

D. Color System

Deliverable What It Is Formats Why It Matters / Example
Primary Palette 2-4 core brand colors that define the brand's visual signature. Each color specified in all color models: Pantone (print standard), CMYK (4-color print), RGB (screen), HEX (web/digital). ASE (Adobe Swatch), Pantone reference numbers, documented in brand guidelines Slack: 4 primary colors (pink, blue, green, yellow) down from 11. Mastercard: red, yellow, orange. The New School: Parsons Red, black, white.
Secondary Palette 6-12 supporting colors for data visualization, illustration, backgrounds, UI states, accent applications. Designed to complement the primary palette. ASE, Pantone numbers, documented in brand guidelines The New School: 12 secondary colors (7 traditional Pantone + 4 fluorescents + 2 metallics). Cohere: coniferous green, mushroom grey, volcanic black + synthetic hues.
Tint & Gradient Rules Specifications for tints (lighter versions), shades (darker versions), and gradient combinations. Which gradients are allowed, direction, stops. Documented in brand guidelines Channel 4: CSS linear gradients + WebGL 3D gradients as core design element. Mastercard: carefully calibrated orange overlap color.
Color Usage Rules Rules for which colors go where: primary backgrounds, text colors, accent applications, contrast requirements (WCAG accessibility), and forbidden combinations. Documented in brand guidelines Slack's previous 11-color palette failed on non-white backgrounds. The 4-color reduction solved this.
Dark Mode Palette Color adaptations for dark interfaces, ensuring contrast and legibility in inverted schemes. Increasingly standard for digital-first brands. Documented in brand guidelines + Figma component library Satalia: full dual palette (color client-facing + dark monochrome technical-facing).

E. Visual Language & Graphic System

Deliverable What It Is Formats Why It Matters / Example
Graphic Elements / Patterns Distinctive graphic shapes, patterns, or textures derived from the brand mark or core concept. Can be parametric (infinite variations from mathematical rules) or fixed. AI, SVG, PDF + source files (Figma, After Effects) Mastercard: parametric patterns built on mathematical principles from the mark. Slack: pill and droplet shapes extracted from logo for icons and illustrations.
Icon Set Custom icon library (typically 50-200+ icons) designed in the brand's visual language. Consistent stroke weight, corner radius, grid, and style. SVG, PNG (multiple sizes), Figma component library, icon font file Cohere: line-drawn icons inspired by Voronoi language, designed within a 24px grid. Calendly: custom icon library derived from logo structure.
Illustration Style Guidelines for illustration: style (line, flat, 3D, hand-drawn), color use, subject matter, level of detail. Often includes sample illustrations or commissioned artwork. AI, SVG, PNG + style guide in brand book Calendly: line-based illustrations by Mike Lemanski, including "Caly" squirrel mascot. Mastercard: parametric generative illustrations.
Photography Direction Art direction for photography: subjects, composition, lighting, color grading, cropping rules, model selection criteria. Sample mood boards and reference images. PDF (within brand book) + sample/reference images (JPEG) ERM: nature-based imagery abstracted through refractive lens. Calendly: "lively, authentic imagery reflecting real moments." Cohere: "new nature" combining organic and synthetic.
Image Treatment / Filters Proprietary visual treatments applied to photography: overlays, duotones, refractive effects, halftone, cropping masks. Creates a signature look. Photoshop actions, Lightroom presets, documented process ERM: tonal range broken into concentric patterns. These treatments make any photo instantly recognizable as the brand.
Data Visualization Style Guidelines for charts, graphs, infographics: which chart types to use, color mapping, label styles, axis treatments, annotation style. Documented in brand book + Figma components Critical for a consulting brand. Ensures every chart in a presentation looks like it belongs to the same family.

04 — Quality Bar

What Makes Pentagram Different from Every Other Agency

Regular Agency

  • Account manager runs the relationship; designers are interchangeable
  • Strategy and design are separate departments with different people
  • Logo presented in isolation, system built after approval
  • Template-based process: same methodology for every client
  • Guidelines delivered as a PDF and forgotten
  • Markup-driven pricing; junior designers do the work
  • CEO or managing director makes business decisions
  • Creative director may never meet the client

Pentagram

  • A named partner (world-class designer) leads every project personally, from pitch to delivery
  • Strategy and design happen simultaneously, by the same person; they reject "strategy as words on a whiteboard"
  • Logo and system are designed together as one organism; the mark generates the system
  • Every project gets a bespoke process tailored to the problem
  • Guidelines delivered as living systems: Figma libraries, parametric tools, custom plugins, interactive web portals
  • Equal-ownership partnership; all 19 partners are practicing designers, not managers
  • No CEO, no shareholders, no external investors. Partners share profits equally.
  • The person who presents the work IS the person who made the work

"Pentagram is the only major design studio where the owners of the business are the creators of the work and serve as the primary contact for every client."

-- pentagram.com/about

The Quality Signals

  • Mathematical precision: Mastercard's patterns built on mathematical principles from the mark itself. Not decorative; derived from the logo's geometry.
  • Parametric systems: Channel 4's toolkit generates infinite variations from rules, not templates. The system creates itself.
  • Custom tools: Cohere received bespoke Figma plugins and Cinema 4D tools. Not just assets: tools to create future assets.
  • Bespoke typefaces: The New School, Cohere, Warner Bros., Channel 4 all received custom-commissioned typefaces. The font IS the brand.
  • Reductive discipline: Slack went from 11 colors to 4. Mastercard dropped its name entirely. Pentagram removes until only the essential remains.
  • Cultural longevity: Shakespeare in the Park: 30 consecutive seasons. London Design Festival: annual redesigns since 2008. These systems are built to last decades.
  • Heritage respect: Warner Bros. was "recalibration, not reinvention." Citi preserved the script heritage. Pentagram evolves; they don't erase.

06 — Priority Map

What a Solo Consultant Actually Needs

Not everything on the Pentagram list applies equally. Here's the priority ranking for a fractional CPO brand, with Pentagram quality standards applied to what matters most.

Tier 1: Non-Negotiable (Revenue Impact)

  • Logo system (primary + symbol + reversed) -- your visual signature on every interaction
  • Typography system (primary + secondary typeface, full scale) -- 80% of brand recognition is typographic
  • Color system (primary 3-4 + secondary 4-6 colors, all specs) -- instant recognition
  • Business card (premium stock, thoughtful printing) -- you hand this to decision-makers
  • Presentation template (30+ master slides) -- used in every client meeting and pitch
  • Proposal template -- the document that closes deals
  • Website design -- the first thing anyone checks
  • Email signature -- seen 500+ times per month
  • Brand guidelines (40+ pages) -- the rulebook that keeps everything consistent

Tier 2: High Value (Professional Polish)

  • Case study template -- proof pack presentation
  • Document templates (report, brief, SOW) -- every document that leaves the brand
  • Social media templates (LinkedIn focus) -- publishing authority
  • Logo animation -- video calls, presentations, content
  • Photography direction -- headshot style, imagery rules
  • Letterhead (digital e-letterhead) -- formal correspondence
  • Invoice template -- professional billing
  • OG images + favicons -- link sharing presence

Tier 3: When Ready (Scale & Events)

  • Conference signage templates -- speaking engagements
  • Motion principles & templates -- video content
  • Icon set -- if building a product or content platform
  • Illustration style -- thought leadership content
  • Notebook / physical stationery -- premium client gifts
  • Brand architecture map -- when service packages are finalized
  • Data visualization style -- client dashboards and reports
  • Figma component library -- when working with design contractors
