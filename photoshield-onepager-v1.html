The Analog Loophole

For decades, security professionals struggled with the gap between the end-point device and the intended end-user (the "Analog Loophole"). No matter how much is invested in cyber security technology, data in transit will eventually need to be displayed on a screen visible to the entitled end user. At that point it is vulnerable to analog means (i.e., cameras and phones).

At no time in history has this Analog Loophole posed a bigger problem for enterprises who now need to demonstrate that they have taken prudent measures to address the new hybrid work model.

Photo Shield solves this problem by preventing users from intentionally or unintentionally disclosing confidential company information
